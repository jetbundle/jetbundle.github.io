noncommutative physics

landau 3–4 backbone, one algebraic deformation from the classical spine

this page assumes you have already walked the unified classical road: symplectic phase space, Poisson brackets and Hamiltonian flow, variational action and Noether data on manifolds, minkowski structure, gauge potentials and curvature, and optionally the bundle connection picture. the physical story of landau–lifshitz volumes 3 and 4 is then read as a single forced upgrade: keep the symplectic and variational origin of dynamics, but replace the commutative algebra of classical observables with an operator algebra whose commutators shadow Poisson brackets.

the deformation rule, stated once and reused everywhere it applies, is $\{f,g\}\mapsto \frac{1}{i\hbar}[\hat f,\hat g]$ on a domain of observables where both sides make sense. ordering choices and domain issues are part of the physics, not bugs in the exposition. every arc below traces a consequence: finite-dimensional quantum mechanics, spin and representation theory, relativistic wave equations, field quantization and a QED-shaped skeleton, global quantization when chartwise canonical rules break, worldsheets and conformal data as the same variational DNA in $1{+}1$, then closure and honest pointers where the deformation is not yet finished (UV, full rigorous QFT, measurement philosophy).

convention: lorentzian signature matches the classical page, mostly minus $\eta=\mathrm{diag}(-1,1,1,1)$, $x^0=ct$; $\hbar$ is explicit in commutators; field normalizations follow heaviside–lorentz style where QED-style formulas appear.

deformation: Poisson algebra meets measurement

the classical index treats mechanics as a story about composition (symmetries, categories), selection (stationary action), and bookkeeping (multilinear objects, forms, bundles). once phase space is symplectic, observables form a commutative poisson algebra and every pure state is morally a point evaluation: sharp joint values cost nothing algebraically. quantum mechanics is the same spine with one lie-theoretic edit: the poisson bracket is shadowed by a commutator bracket on a noncommutative $\ast$-algebra, and measurement is read through spectral measures instead of pointwise evaluation. this arc makes that edit as explicit as the opening sections of the classical page make monoids, actions, and euler–lagrange.

throughout, keep the classical dictionary in view: $f,g$ on $T^*Q$ commute under pointwise product; $\{f,g\}$ is the infinitesimal twin of hamiltonian shearing. after deformation, $\hat f,\hat g$ need not commute; $\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}[\hat f,\hat g]$ is the quantum twin of $\{f,g\}$ on the largest subalgebra where the identification is consistent. the double slit and uncertainty are not metaphors layered on top—they are the failure of commutative spectral calculus that the classical page never had to confront.

symplectic coordinates and poisson axioms (classical spine). on an open chart of phase space with darboux coordinates $(q^1,\ldots,q^n,p_1,\ldots,p_n)$, use $z=(q,p)\in\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ and the standard symplectic matrix

$$ J = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & I_n \\ -I_n & 0 \end{pmatrix},\qquad \omega(z,z') = z^{\mathsf T} J z' . $$

for smooth observables $f,g$, the poisson bracket is

$$ \{f,g\} = \sum_{i=1}^n\left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial q^i}\frac{\partial g}{\partial p_i} - \frac{\partial f}{\partial p_i}\frac{\partial g}{\partial q^i} \right) = \omega(X_f,X_g), $$

where $X_f$ is the hamiltonian vector field defined by $\iota_{X_f}\omega=\mathrm{d}f$. it satisfies the following axioms for all $f,g,h\in C^\infty$ and constants $a,b$:

  1. P1 (bilinearity). $\{af+bg,h\}=a\{f,h\}+b\{g,h\}$ and similarly in the second slot.
  2. P2 (antisymmetry). $\{g,f\}=-\{f,g\}$.
  3. P3 (jacobi). $\{f,\{g,h\}\}+\{g,\{h,f\}\}+\{h,\{f,g\}\}=0$.
  4. P4 (leibniz). $\{fg,h\}=f\{g,h\}+\{f,h\}g$.

given a hamiltonian $H$, time evolution of observables along physical trajectories obeys

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}f}{\mathrm{d}t}=\{f,H\}. $$

a pure classical state $x\in M$ induces an algebra homomorphism $\mathrm{ev}_x:C^\infty(M,\mathbb{R})\to\mathbb{R}$, $f\mapsto f(x)$, compatible with pointwise product: $\mathrm{ev}_x(fg)=\mathrm{ev}_x(f)\mathrm{ev}_x(g)$. commutativity of the product is what allows, at least in idealized language, arbitrarily sharp simultaneous values for any finite list of independent observables whose level sets meet transversely. the quantum upgrade replaces this by spectral projectors that need not commute.

sharp values and joint spectra

postulates (quantum structural, finite-level). fix a separable complex Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$.

  1. Q1 (observables). a (real) observable is a self-adjoint operator $A$ with dense domain $\mathcal{D}(A)\subset\mathcal{H}$ (in applications one constructs a common core $\mathcal{D}_0$ where algebraic identities are checked, then closes to self-adjoint extensions).
  2. Q2 (pure states). pure states are rays: nonzero $\psi\in\mathcal{H}$ with $\psi\sim \lambda\psi$ for $\lambda\in\mathbb{C}^\times$ identified.
  3. Q3 (mixed states). general states are density operators: positive trace-class operators $\rho$ with $\mathrm{Tr}\,\rho=1$.
  4. Q4 (expectations). $\langle A\rangle_\psi=\langle\psi,A\psi\rangle/\langle\psi,\psi\rangle$ for pure states; $\langle A\rangle_\rho=\mathrm{Tr}(\rho A)$ when $A\rho$ is trace-class (e.g. $A$ bounded or $\rho$ finite-rank on the relevant subspace).

spectral theorem (functional form). to each self-adjoint $A$ corresponds a unique projection-valued measure $E_A$ on the Borel $\sigma$-algebra of $\mathbb{R}$ such that

$$ A = \int_{\mathbb{R}} \lambda\,\mathrm{d}E_A(\lambda), $$

with the integral understood in the strong sense on $\mathcal{D}(A)$. for every bounded Borel $g$,

$$ g(A) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} g(\lambda)\,\mathrm{d}E_A(\lambda) $$

defines a bounded operator. the born rule assigns to a Borel set $\Delta\subset\mathbb{R}$ the outcome probability

$$ \mathbb{P}_\psi(A\in\Delta)=\frac{\|E_A(\Delta)\psi\|^2}{\|\psi\|^2},\qquad \mathbb{P}_\rho(A\in\Delta)=\mathrm{Tr}\bigl(\rho\,E_A(\Delta)\bigr), $$

for pure and mixed states respectively (under standard trace-class hypotheses).

lemma (PVM bookkeeping). the family $\{E_A(\Delta)\}$ satisfies $E_A(\emptyset)=0$, $E_A(\mathbb{R})=I$, $E_A(\Delta_1\cap\Delta_2)=E_A(\Delta_1)E_A(\Delta_2)$, and for pairwise disjoint $\Delta_n$, $E_A(\bigcup_n\Delta_n)=\sum_n E_A(\Delta_n)$ in the strong operator topology. these are the axioms of a resolution of the identity; they are the quantum replacement for pulling back borel sets through classical $f:M\to\mathbb{R}$.

definition (joint measurability). self-adjoint $A,B$ are jointly measurable if their spectral measures strongly commute:

$$ [E_A(\Delta_1),E_B(\Delta_2)]=0\quad\text{for all Borel }\Delta_1,\Delta_2\subset\mathbb{R}. $$

equivalently, $f(A)$ and $g(B)$ commute for all bounded borel $f,g$. under standard regularity (bounded $A,B$, or polynomially bounded on a joint core with essential self-adjointness of polynomials), joint measurability is equivalent to $[A,B]=0$ on a dense subspace invariant under both operators’ bounded functional calculus.

proposition (simultaneous diagonalization, statement). if $A,B$ are commuting self-adjoint operators on $\mathcal{H}$, there exists a unitary map $\mathcal{H}\cong\int^\oplus \mathcal{H}(\lambda,\mu)\,\mathrm{d}\nu(\lambda,\mu)$ diagonalizing both: $A$ acts as multiplication by $\lambda$, $B$ by $\mu$ on each fiber. in particular, when spectra are discrete and multiplicities finite, there is an orthonormal basis of simultaneous eigenvectors.

canonical commutation relations (CCR) on a core. in one dimension, position and momentum are modeled by symmetric operators on $\mathcal{D}_0\subset L^2(\mathbb{R},\mathrm{d}x)$ with

$$ [\hat x,\hat p]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\mathbb{I},\qquad \hat x\psi(x)=x\psi(x),\qquad \hat p\psi(x)=-\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\psi'(x). $$

lemma (no sharp joint $(x,p)$ in $L^2$). there is no nonzero $\psi\in L^2(\mathbb{R})$ satisfying both $\hat x\psi=x_0\psi$ and $\hat p\psi=p_0\psi$ for finite $x_0,p_0\in\mathbb{R}$.

proof. the equation $\hat p\psi=p_0\psi$ forces $\psi(x)=C\,\exp(\mathrm{i}p_0 x/\hbar)$ a.e., which has constant modulus $|C|^2$ and cannot be square-integrable unless $C=0$. separately, $\hat x\psi=x_0\psi$ would require $\psi$ supported at $x_0$ (a delta distribution), which is not an element of $L^2$. thus the classical idealization of a point in phase space cannot be realized as a simultaneous pair of proper eigenvectors in the schrödinger representation.

remark (continuous spectrum and rigged spaces). generalized eigenvectors (plane waves, deltas) live in distributional extensions (gelfand triples); the operational content remains the projection-valued measure on $\mathcal{H}$. discrete spectra (finite-dimensional subspaces, bound states) are the setting where “sharp values” look most like finite-dimensional linear algebra, but the joint measurability criterion is always the commuting of spectral projectors, not the classical existence of a point in $T^*Q$.

double slit, interference, which-path

work in a two-dimensional schematic: transverse coordinate $y$ on a screen, two slits labeled $1,2$. after preparation, the which-slit degree of freedom is modeled as a two-dimensional subspace $\mathbb{C}^2=\mathrm{span}\{|1\rangle,|2\rangle\}$ with $\langle i|j\rangle=\delta_{ij}$. a pure pre-slit transverse state is

$$ |\psi_{\mathrm{slit}}\rangle=\alpha|1\rangle+\beta|2\rangle,\qquad |\alpha|^2+|\beta|^2=1. $$

propagation from slit $j$ to a point $y$ on the screen contributes a complex amplitude $K_j(y)$ (a green function / propagator evaluated between slit and detector in a fixed approximation). the total amplitude at $y$ is the coherent sum

$$ \psi(y)=\alpha K_1(y)+\beta K_2(y). $$

lemma (born composition). the detection density is proportional to

$$ |\psi(y)|^2 =|\alpha|^2|K_1|^2+|\beta|^2|K_2|^2 +2\operatorname{Re}\bigl(\alpha^*\beta\,K_1^*K_2\bigr). $$

proof. expand $|\alpha K_1+\beta K_2|^2$ and group terms.

the third term is the interference term; it vanishes if either amplitude $\alpha^*\beta$ is zero or if the relative phase of $K_1^*K_2$ is washed out. in the far-field approximation with equal slit–screen optical path length moduli and small diffraction envelope, one often writes $K_j(y)\propto \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\phi_j(y)}$ with slowly varying modulus; then the interference oscillates as $\cos(\phi_2(y)-\phi_1(y)+\arg(\beta/\alpha))$.

worked example (symmetric slits). take $|\alpha|=|\beta|=1/\sqrt{2}$ and $|K_1(y)|=|K_2(y)|=:A(y)>0$ with $K_1^*K_2=A(y)^2\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\delta(y)}$. then

$$ |\psi(y)|^2 = A(y)^2\bigl(1+\cos\delta(y)\bigr)=2A(y)^2\cos^2\bigl(\delta(y)/2\bigr), $$

exhibiting perfect fringe visibility when $\delta$ sweeps a range much larger than $2\pi$ across the screen.

which-path coupling (tensor product model). adjoin an apparatus with orthonormal readout states $|e_1\rangle,|e_2\rangle$ in $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{m}}$ and entangle:

$$ |\Psi\rangle=\alpha|1\rangle\otimes|e_1\rangle+\beta|2\rangle\otimes|e_2\rangle. $$

the reduced density matrix on the slit space after tracing out $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{m}}$ is

$$ \rho_{\mathrm{slit}} =\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathrm{m}}|\Psi\rangle\langle\Psi| =|\alpha|^2|1\rangle\langle 1|+|\beta|^2|2\rangle\langle 2| $$

whenever $\langle e_1|e_2\rangle=0$ (orthogonal which-path records). expanding the same amplitudes with this $\rho$ replaces $|\psi|^2$ by an incoherent sum: cross terms vanish because coherences $\langle 1|\rho|2\rangle$ are zero.

lemma (two-level visibility). for any $2\times 2$ hermitian density matrix $\rho$ in the $\{|1\rangle,|2\rangle\}$ basis, define fringe visibility from off-diagonal magnitude: $V=2|\rho_{12}|/(\rho_{11}+\rho_{22})$ when the denominator is positive. pure superpositions $|\langle 1|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|2\rangle|$ maximize $|V|$ for fixed diagonal weights; the partial trace above sets $\rho_{12}=0$ while preserving $\rho_{11}=|\alpha|^2$, $\rho_{22}=|\beta|^2$.

remark (complementarity as algebra). which-path information is correlation with a commuting subalgebra of the apparatus; complete distinguishability orthogonalizes the records, erasing coherences in the reduced state. interference reappears only when the joint state remains entangled in a basis where both slits contribute to the same off-diagonal of the relevant reduced density operator. this is the same structural obstruction as noncommuting spectral measures: the classical phase-space point cannot encode both sharp slit label and maximal fringe contrast in one commutative probability space without enlarging the sample space (here, tensor product structure).

dirac map: Poisson bracket to commutator

definition (dirac quantization map, local form). fix a darboux chart on $M=T^*Q$ and let $\mathcal{P}\subset C^\infty(M)$ be a real vector subspace closed under poisson bracket (a lie subalgebra under $\{\cdot,\cdot\}$). a quantization map is a linear assignment $f\mapsto \hat f$ from $\mathcal{P}$ to self-adjoint (or hermitian) operators on $\mathcal{H}$ such that, on a specified dense domain,

$$ \widehat{\{f,g\}} = \frac{1}{\mathrm{i}\hbar}\,[\hat f,\hat g] $$

whenever $f,g,\{f,g\}\in\mathcal{P}$. this is a lie algebra homomorphism from $(\mathcal{P},\{\cdot,\cdot\})$ to $(\mathrm{Op}(\mathcal{H}),\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}[\cdot,\cdot])$ after restricting to the image.

proposition (heisenberg algebra is consistent). let $\mathcal{P}_2$ be real polynomials in $(q,p)$ of degree at most two. there exists an quantization map on $\mathcal{P}_2$ with $\hat q,\hat p$ the standard operators and $\widehat{q^2}=\hat q^2$, $\widehat{p^2}=\hat p^2$, $\widehat{qp}=\frac12(\hat q\hat p+\hat p\hat q)$, satisfying the exact dirac identity for all pairs in $\mathcal{P}_2$.

verification (quadratic sample). classically $\{q^2,p\}=2q$. quantum mechanically, using $[\hat q,\hat p]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\mathbb{I}$,

$$ [\hat q^2,\hat p] =\hat q[\hat q,\hat p]+[\hat q,\hat p]\hat q =2\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\hat q, \qquad\text{hence}\qquad \frac{1}{\mathrm{i}\hbar}[\hat q^2,\hat p]=2\hat q=\widehat{\{q^2,p\}}. $$

similar checks cover all generators of $\mathcal{P}_2$; the symmetric (weyl) ordering for $\widehat{qp}$ is forced if one insists on hermitian $\widehat{qp}$ and consistency with linear assignments.

theorem (Groenewold–van Hove, statement). there is no linear quantization map from the full lie algebra of all polynomials in $(q,p)$ to operators satisfying the exact dirac identity for every pair and agreeing with the schrödinger representation on $\mathcal{P}_2$. in short, no universal polynomial quantization functor exists; ordering is extra data.

remark (deformation quantization / Moyal, one line of algebra). the weyl symbol map identifies polynomials with operators; the product of symbols is deformed to the moyal star-product $f\star g$ with

$$ f\star g - g\star f = \mathrm{i}\hbar\,\{f,g\} + O(\hbar^3) $$

in the semiclassical expansion for polynomials (the $\hbar^2$ term already encodes ordering choices). this packages the dirac map inside an associative but noncommutative product on a function space, parallel to how the classical page packages dynamics inside the poisson bracket’s derivation property P4.

leading deformation identities (boxed for reuse). matching the classical canonical brackets,

$$ \boxed{\; \{q^i,p_j\}=\delta^i_j \;\longleftrightarrow\; [\hat q^i,\hat p_j]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\delta^i_j,\qquad [\hat q^i,\hat q^j]=[\hat p_i,\hat p_j]=0 \;} $$

and for dynamics, the dirac analogy continues in the heisenberg equation derived below: $\mathrm{d}\hat f/\mathrm{d}t=\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}[\hat H,\hat f]$ versus $\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}t=\{H,f\}$.

robertson uncertainty from commutators

definition (dispersion). for a self-adjoint $A$ and a unit vector $\psi\in\mathcal{D}(A^2)$, set $\langle A\rangle=\langle\psi,A\psi\rangle$ and

$$ (\Delta A)^2=\langle(A-\langle A\rangle\mathbb{I})^2\rangle=\|A_0\psi\|^2,\qquad A_0=A-\langle A\rangle\mathbb{I}. $$

theorem (Robertson). let $A,B$ be self-adjoint and $\psi\in\mathcal{D}(AB)\cap\mathcal{D}(BA)$ with $\|\psi\|=1$. then

$$ \boxed{\; \Delta A\,\Delta B\;\geq\;\frac{1}{2}\,\bigl|\langle [A,B]\rangle\bigr| \;} $$

where $\langle[A,B]\rangle=\langle\psi,[A,B]\psi\rangle$.

proof. with $A_0,B_0$ centered, self-adjointness gives $\langle\psi,[A_0,B_0]\psi\rangle=\langle A_0\psi,B_0\psi\rangle-\langle B_0\psi,A_0\psi\rangle =2\mathrm{i}\,\operatorname{Im}\langle A_0\psi,B_0\psi\rangle$. hence $|\langle[A_0,B_0]\rangle|\leq 2|\langle A_0\psi,B_0\psi\rangle|$. Cauchy–Schwarz yields $|\langle A_0\psi,B_0\psi\rangle|\leq\|A_0\psi\|\,\|B_0\psi\|=\Delta A\,\Delta B$. finally $[A_0,B_0]=[A,B]$ because scalars commute. combine to obtain the claim.

corollary (Heisenberg–Kennard for one mode). for $[\hat x,\hat p]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\mathbb{I}$,

$$ \Delta x\,\Delta p\geq \frac{\hbar}{2}. $$

theorem (Schrödinger / Robertson combined cleanly). with $\{A_0,B_0\}=A_0B_0+B_0A_0$,

$$ (\Delta A)^2(\Delta B)^2 \geq \frac{1}{4}\bigl|\langle[A,B]\rangle\bigr|^2 +\frac{1}{4}\bigl|\langle\{A_0,B_0\}\rangle\bigr|^2. $$

proof. Cauchy–Schwarz on $A_0\psi$ and $B_0\psi$ gives $|\langle A_0B_0\rangle|\leq \Delta A\,\Delta B$. expand $A_0B_0=\tfrac12\{A_0,B_0\}+\tfrac12[A_0,B_0]$; for self-adjoint $A_0,B_0$, $\langle\{A_0,B_0\}\rangle\in\mathbb{R}$ and $\langle[A_0,B_0]\rangle=\langle A_0B_0\rangle-\overline{\langle A_0B_0\rangle}=2\mathrm{i}\,\operatorname{Im}\langle A_0B_0\rangle$, hence $|\langle A_0B_0\rangle|^2 =(\operatorname{Re}\langle A_0B_0\rangle)^2+(\operatorname{Im}\langle A_0B_0\rangle)^2 =\tfrac14\langle\{A_0,B_0\}\rangle^2+\tfrac14|\langle[A,B]\rangle|^2$. combine with Cauchy–Schwarz. robertson follows by dropping the nonnegative anticommutator term.

worked example (gaussian saturates $\Delta x\,\Delta p=\hbar/2$). take the normalized state

$$ \psi(x)=\left(\frac{1}{2\pi\sigma^2}\right)^{1/4}\exp\!\Bigl(-\frac{x^2}{4\sigma^2}\Bigr) \in L^2(\mathbb{R},\mathrm{d}x). $$

then $\Delta x=\sigma$ and a fourier transform gives $\Delta p=\hbar/(2\sigma)$, hence $\Delta x\,\Delta p=\hbar/2$. equality in the robertson step of the proof forces collinearity of $A_0\psi$ and $B_0\psi$; gaussians (translated in $x$ and boosted in momentum by phase) exhaust the minimizers in one dimension.

heisenberg versus schrödinger picture

let $\hat H$ be self-adjoint and time-independent. stone’s theorem guarantees a strongly continuous one-parameter unitary group with generator $\hat H$; write

$$ \hat U(t)=\exp\!\Bigl(-\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}t\hat H\Bigr). $$

Schrödinger picture. states evolve, operators fixed:

$$ |\psi(t)\rangle=\hat U(t)|\psi(0)\rangle,\qquad \mathrm{i}\hbar\,\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}|\psi(t)\rangle=\hat H|\psi(t)\rangle $$

on $\mathcal{D}(\hat H)$.

Heisenberg picture. states fixed, operators evolve:

$$ \hat A_H(t)=\hat U(t)^\dagger\,\hat A\,\hat U(t). $$

lemma (expectation equivalence). for all $t$ and bounded $\hat A$ (more generally on domains preserved by $\hat U(t)$),

$$ \langle\psi(t)|\hat A|\psi(t)\rangle=\langle\psi(0)|\hat A_H(t)|\psi(0)\rangle. $$

proof. substitute $|\psi(t)\rangle=\hat U(t)|\psi(0)\rangle$ and use unitarity $\hat U^\dagger\hat U=I$.

computation (Heisenberg equation). differentiate strongly on a core (formally, product rule):

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\hat A_H(t) =\frac{\mathrm{d}\hat U^\dagger}{\mathrm{d}t}\hat A\hat U+\hat U^\dagger\hat A\frac{\mathrm{d}\hat U}{\mathrm{d}t}. $$

since $\mathrm{d}\hat U/\mathrm{d}t=-\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\hat H\hat U$ and $\mathrm{d}\hat U^\dagger/\mathrm{d}t=\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\hat U^\dagger\hat H$,

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}\hat A_H}{\mathrm{d}t} =\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\hat U^\dagger(\hat H\hat A-\hat A\hat H)\hat U =\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\,[\hat H,\hat A_H(t)]. $$

thus

$$ \boxed{\; \frac{\mathrm{d}\hat A_H}{\mathrm{d}t} =\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\,[\hat H,\hat A_H(t)] \;} $$

on the appropriate domain. this mirrors $\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}t=\{H,f\}$ with the dirac substitution $\{\cdot,\cdot\}\mapsto \frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}[\cdot,\cdot]$ on the lie subalgebra where quantization is exact.

corollary (Ehrenfest, schrödinger form). for $\psi(t)$ solving the schrödinger equation and $\hat A$ time-independent in the schrödinger picture,

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\langle\hat A\rangle =\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\,\langle[\hat H,\hat A]\rangle, $$

with the expectation taken in $|\psi(t)\rangle$. for mixed $\rho(t)=\hat U(t)\rho(0)\hat U(t)^\dagger$, $\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\mathrm{Tr}(\rho\hat A) =\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}\mathrm{Tr}(\rho[\hat H,\hat A])$ under trace-class hypotheses.

interaction picture (pointer). split $\hat H=\hat H_0+\hat V$ and set $\hat U_0(t)=\exp(-\mathrm{i}t\hat H_0/\hbar)$. operators carry free evolution, $\hat A_I(t)=\hat U_0(t)^\dagger\hat A\hat U_0(t)$, while states solve $\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\partial_t|\psi_I(t)\rangle=\hat V_I(t)|\psi_I(t)\rangle$ with $\hat V_I(t)=\hat U_0(t)^\dagger\hat V\hat U_0(t)$. this is the bookkeeping behind time-dependent perturbation theory and dyson series in later arcs.

worked example (harmonic oscillator ladder in Heisenberg). for $\hat H=\hbar\omega(\hat a^\dagger\hat a+\tfrac12)$ with $[\hat a,\hat a^\dagger]=\mathbb{I}$, the Heisenberg equations $\mathrm{d}\hat a/\mathrm{d}t=\frac{\mathrm{i}}{\hbar}[\hat H,\hat a]$ and $[\hat H,\hat a]=-\hbar\omega\hat a$ give

$$ \hat a_H(t)=\mathrm{e}^{-\mathrm{i}\omega t}\hat a(0),\qquad \hat a^\dagger_H(t)=\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\omega t}\hat a^\dagger(0), $$

so $\hat x_H(t)\propto \hat a_H+\hat a_H^\dagger$ oscillates at frequency $\omega$ while vector states in the schrödinger picture need not move if prepared in an energy eigenstate—two faces of the same unitary evolution, exactly as the classical page’s hamiltonian flow can be read either on observables or on initial points once a symplectomorphism is fixed.

canonical quantization: Heisenberg algebra to Schrödinger equation

this arc closes the finite-degree-of-freedom story before spin and fields complicate representation theory: start from canonical commutators, justify uniqueness of the irreducible unitary representation (stone–von neumann), build $L^2$ wave mechanics, state the born rule, and recover classical mechanics in the WKB / Hamilton–Jacobi limit tied to action–angle and hamilton–jacobi on the classical side.

canonical commutators and weyl form

the heisenberg relations $[\hat q_i,\hat p_j]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\delta_{ij}$ exponentiate to weyl relations for unitary phase operators; this avoids domain pathologies in stating uniqueness. the classical bracket $\{q^i,p_j\}=\delta^i_j$ is the leading term of the commutator in $\hbar$.

stone–von neumann rigidity

for finite degrees of freedom, irreducible unitary representations of the weyl algebra are unique up to unitary equivalence. this is the quantum rigidity that has no classical analogue: darboux charts are flexible symplectically, but the irreducible Hilbert-space realization is essentially unique.

Hilbert space, domains, self-adjointness

the schrödinger representation uses $\mathcal{H}=L^2(\mathbb{R}^n,\mathrm{d}^nq)$ with $\hat q$ multiplication and $\hat p=-\mathrm{i}\hbar\nabla$ on schwartz-class cores. physically acceptable hamiltonians require self-adjoint or essentially self-adjoint extensions (free particle, harmonic oscillator, hydrogen) so that unitary time evolution exists.

states, density matrices, born rule

pure states are rays in $\mathcal{H}$; general states are density matrices $\rho$ with $\mathrm{Tr}\,\rho=1$, $\rho\ge 0$. expectations are $\langle A\rangle=\mathrm{Tr}(\rho\hat A)$. the born rule ties probabilities to spectral measures. compare to classical mixed states as probability measures on phase space; the quantum linearity of superposition is new.

schrödinger equation and unitary evolution

the abstract statement is $\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\partial_t\psi=\hat H\psi$ with $\psi\in\mathcal{H}$. formally $\psi(t)=\hat U(t)\psi(0)$. stationary states reduce to the spectral problem for $\hat H$.

deformed variational principle for the wavefunction

the schrödinger equation can be derived from a variational principle on complex fields (time-dependent euler–lagrange in field form), preserving the variational lineage from classical action principles. path-integral formulations push the same idea to infinite-dimensional integration (heuristic until measure is specified).

WKB and Hamilton–Jacobi recovery

the ansatz $\psi\sim \exp(\mathrm{i}S/\hbar)$ yields at leading order in $\hbar$ the Hamilton–Jacobi equation for $S(q,t)$, linking straight back to the classical generating-function picture in hamilton–jacobi and integrable structure. classical trajectories live in the stationary-phase support of amplitudes.

symmetries, angular momentum, spin

classical continuous symmetries on phase space generate conserved quantities via Noether’s story. in quantum mechanics, symmetries are implemented by (projective) unitary operators; stone’s theorem ties one-parameter groups to self-adjoint generators. spatial rotations produce the $\mathfrak{so}(3)$ commutator algebra; the universal cover $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ forces half-integer spin and connects to the double-cover story already visible for SO(3) and rigid body.

symmetries as projective unitary representations

wigner’s framework: symmetries are ray maps preserving transition probabilities; they lift to unitary or antiunitary operators. time-reversal and certain discrete symmetries involve the latter; continuous connected symmetries are typically unitary.

stone’s theorem and generators

strongly continuous one-parameter unitary groups $U(s)$ have self-adjoint generators $A$ with $U(s)=\exp(-\mathrm{i}sA/\hbar)$ (conventions vary). this is the quantum replacement for the classical hamiltonian flow generated by a moment map.

so(3) algebra, ladders, spectra

components $J_i$ satisfy $[J_i,J_j]=\mathrm{i}\hbar\,\epsilon_{ijk}J_k$. ladder operators raise and lower $J_z$ eigenvalues; $J^2$ Casimir labels total angular momentum quantum number. the classical vector $\mathbf{L}=\mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{p}$ obeys the same bracket structure at the Poisson level.

SU(2) covering SO(3), half-integer spin

projective representations of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ lift to linear representations of $\mathrm{SU}(2)$. half-integer spin representations are faithful on $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ but only projective on spatial rotations, matching the sign ambiguity of spinors under $2\pi$ rotation.

Pauli matrices, Bloch sphere, Stern–Gerlach

spin-$\tfrac12$ uses pauli matrices with $[\sigma_j,\sigma_k]=2\mathrm{i}\epsilon_{jkl}\sigma_l$. pure states form the Bloch sphere; Stern–Gerlach is the two-outcome noncommutative measurement prototype.

tensor products and composite systems

composite systems use $\mathcal{H}_1\otimes\mathcal{H}_2$; entanglement appears as non-factorizable states. this generalizes classical product configuration spaces $Q_1\times Q_2$ from manifold-level mechanics. identical-particle statistics wait for the field arc.

relativistic extension: Klein–Gordon and Dirac

classical four-momentum lives on minkowski space as in flat spacetime kinematics. quantizing the mass-shell constraint $p_\mu p^\mu=m^2c^2$ yields the Klein–Gordon equation; linearizing in a first-order way compatible with spin forces the Clifford algebra and the Dirac equation. minimal coupling $p_\mu\to p_\mu-qA_\mu$ matches the classical gauge minimal coupling from potentials and field strength.

from mass shell to Klein–Gordon

the scalar wave equation $(\Box+m^2)\phi=0$ follows from promoting $p_\mu$ to operators on spacetime. negative-frequency modes signal the need for field reinterpretation (particle / antiparticle) and careful definition of conserved currents.

Clifford algebra and Dirac equation

dirac matrices satisfy $\{\gamma^\mu,\gamma^\nu\}=2\eta^{\mu\nu}$. the Dirac operator $\mathrm{i}\gamma^\mu\partial_\mu-m$ linearizes the KG operator squared. spinor fields carry representations of $\mathrm{Spin}(1,3)$.

Lorentz covariance and minimal coupling

covariance fixes the form of couplings to $A_\mu$; nonrelativistic reduction recovers pauli dynamics with g-factor $2$ at leading order for weak fields. this is the same geometric minimal-coupling story as classical electrodynamics, now on operator-valued spinors.

antiparticles, fall to center, regularity

relativistic coulomb-type problems exhibit regimes where classical singular potentials would collapse; the quantum field viewpoint regulates behavior via pair creation thresholds and vacuum stability conditions (landau-level themes, stated here as pointers to Vol. 4 calculations).

field quantization and QED-shaped skeleton

classical fields on spacetime appear as sections and forms in flat spacetime SR and ED; gauge redundancy and cohomology of $F$ were already global issues. quantization promotes field amplitudes to operator-valued distributions; bosonic commutators and fermionic anticommutators build Fock space; minimal coupling and interaction picture produce the QED interaction term; wick contraction and diagrams encode perturbation theory. the Ehresmann connection picture is the classical geometric spine behind gauge fixing and bundle-level thinking.

CCR, CAR, and Fock space

bosonic modes obey canonical commutation relations; fermionic modes obey anticommutation. Fock space builds total Hilbert space from one-particle space via symmetrized or antisymmetrized tensor powers, enforcing bose / fermi statistics algebraically.

operator-valued distributions and smearing

fields $\hat\phi(f)$ are defined against test functions $f$ because pointwise multiplication is ill-defined. this parallels switching from classical smooth fields to distributional sources in maxwell theory, but now operators replace $c$-numbers.

free photon and electron fields

maxwell quantization yields photon creation / annihilation operators in transverse polarizations; dirac quantization yields electron / positron operators. vacuum choice and normal ordering fix zero-point subtraction conventions.

minimal coupling, gauge fixing, QED interaction

the interaction hamiltonian (or lagrangian density) couples $\bar\psi\gamma^\mu\psi$ to $A_\mu$. gauge fixing (landau, feynman, coulomb) is required for perturbation theory while preserving physical equivalence classes of observables.

interaction picture and S-matrix (formal)

the dyson series expresses time evolution as time-ordered exponentials of the interaction in the interaction picture. unitarity of the S-matrix is the formal constraint; IR divergences in massless theories appear as incompleteness of naive fock space for scattering.

wick, diagrams, UV as residual issue

wick’s theorem reduces time-ordered products to contractions; feynman diagrams bookkeep them. one-loop divergences preview renormalization: the deformation to noncommutative fields does not by itself fix short-distance singularities of interaction distributions.

global geometric quantization

when configuration space is not $\mathbb{R}^n$ or when polarizations are twisted, schrödinger quantization in a single chart fails to be invariant or globally defined. geometric quantization starts from a symplectic manifold $(M,\omega)$ (think generalized phase space, as in symplectic geometry and geometric mechanics), builds a prequantum line bundle with curvature $\omega$, imposes integrality tied to cohomological periods, then chooses a polarization to halve degrees of freedom. bohr–sommerfeld rules reappear as geometric conditions with maslov corrections, linking to action–angle classical intuition.

limits of chartwise Schrödinger quantization

ordering ambiguities and lack of canonical transformation equivariance force a coordinate-free replacement. darboux holds locally, but global topology of $T^*Q$ matters.

prequantum line bundle and integrality

prequantization requires a hermitian line bundle with unitary connection whose curvature is $\omega$. weil integrality demands $\frac{1}{2\pi\hbar}\int_\Sigma\omega\in\mathbb{Z}$ for closed surfaces $\Sigma$. this is the geometric origin of quantization of certain classical periods.

polarizations and half-form correction

real polarizations (lagrangian foliations) pick position- or momentum-like representations; kähler polarizations link to holomorphic / bargmann–fock pictures. metaplectic correction adjusts phases and zero-point shifts (maslov indices).

Bohr–Sommerfeld, Maslov, EBK

semiclassical quantization conditions on lagrangian tori combine action integrals with maslov phases. this closes the loop to integrable classical systems and torus quantization.

dirac constraints and gauge reduction (pointer)

gauge systems with constraints require dirac quantization or BRST methods; classical constraint geometry appears in frobenius and nonholonomic discussion. full treatment belongs to dedicated field-theory texts.

complex structures, sigma models, worldsheets

the classical particle action $\int L\,\mathrm{d}t$ on $TQ$ generalizes to maps $\Sigma\to X$ from a worldsheet (or worldline with internal geometry). in $1{+}1$ dimensions, conformal symmetry is infinite-dimensional; the virasoro algebra is the quantum central extension of conformal killing symmetries. CFT data (primary fields, OPE, modular $S$-matrix) package operator algebras that replace hamiltonian time evolution as the organizing principle. this arc is intentionally lean: it shows the same variational and deformation philosophy applied to worldsheets, not a full string course.

Riemann surfaces and worldsheet actions

complex coordinates on $\Sigma$ give conformal structure; string and sigma-model actions are reparametrization invariant with enhanced symmetry in 2D. classical solutions are harmonic maps in relevant gauges.

conformal symmetry and Virasoro algebra

modes of the stress tensor satisfy $[L_m,L_n]=(m-n)L_{m+n}+\frac{c}{12}m(m^2-1)\delta_{m+n,0}$ with central charge $c$. the central term is a pure quantum effect breaking naive classical scale symmetry.

CFT data: OPE, modular invariance (outline)

operator product expansions define short-distance algebra of fields. on a torus, modular invariance of the partition function constrains spectra; the verlinde formula links fusion to modular matrices (stated as pointer).

nonlinear sigma models and target geometry

pullback metrics and $B$-fields on the target define couplings on the worldsheet; connections to geometric quantization of loop spaces appear when one treats boundary conditions and branes (pointer only).

interpretation, closure, what this page does not finish

the narrative thread is one deformation of the classical observable algebra, anchored in landau’s quantum and relativistic volumes, built on the symplectic and variational foundations of the classical index. the following subsections collect the dictionary between classical and quantum idioms, state the operational role of operator algebras, and list honest omissions.

one deformation, many faces

poisson bracket becomes commutator; phase-space points become rays or density matrices; liouville evolution becomes unitary schrödinger evolution; classical superposition of probabilities becomes interference of amplitudes; sharp trajectories become emergent in WKB / classical limits tied to hamilton–jacobi.

operator algebras and the operational core

$C^\ast$-algebraic formulations emphasize that all measurable content lives in expectation values and spectral measures. this viewpoint unifies finite and infinite systems at the cost of analytic detail in concrete models.

microlocal QFT on curved spacetime (pointer)

on curved lorentzian manifolds, lack of global poincaré symmetry destroys a preferred vacuum; hadamard states and wavefront-set conditions replace minkowski fock vacuum; stress-energy renormalization feeds back to geometry. connect to classical GR template as semiclassical target.

rigged Hilbert spaces (pointer)

continuous spectra require rigged hilbert spaces (gelfand triples) to treat generalized eigenvectors such as plane waves as honest objects paired with test functions.

supersymmetry as later graded deformation (pointer)

supersymmetry extends the algebra with odd generators; anticommutators join commutators in the deformation package; UV cancellations in susy gauge theories are a further layer beyond this page.

out of scope and next books

this index does not construct interacting QFT rigorously in four dimensions, develop full renormalization group technology, settle measurement interpretation, or treat lattice gauge theory. those are the next deformations and readings after landau 3–4 and the geometric supplements sketched above. when in doubt, return to the classical spine and ask what commutative structure failed, then promote that failure to a commutator.
