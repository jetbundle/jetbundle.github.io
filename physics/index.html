physics

this is a view of my fun adventure to build a working mental image of physics in all of her divine beauty. this page will frequently be updated and changed over time. this is not academic nor bureaucratic advice, and i am neither liable nor responsible for any of the romantic decisions you choose to make or feelings evoked as a consequence of your interaction with my writing for all legal concerns in the present and eternal future. :)

it makes the most sense to start with things that are considered to be basic to the mathematical and physical phenomena greatly detailed by the ideas accumulated up to early 20th century science. you will waste a lot of time and be a circularly reasoning cringe physicist/mathematician if you allow yourself to move on before mastery of the basics, at least before obtaining a conceptual vantage point on the basic notions. don't be like me and retrospectively fill the gaps and come to the realizations that could have been done earlier lol. i've embarrassed myself enough when several things should have been automatic and immediate from proper physico-mathematical training. the meticulous, detailed completion and constant mental revision of landau vol. 1 and 2 should really change how you motivate the ontological and philosophical directions of mathematics. the transcendental presuppositions of least action, geometric symmetry, conservation, and relativistic invariance are enforced by a structured, terse approach to particles/fields and their curved background geometry first and foremost. a principled development of einsteinian relativity in both the physical and mathematical sense is the only correct approach to ALL modern geometric theories, namely: Kaluza–Klein, Yang–Mills, spin geometry, and GUTs.

whether you have walked the classical arc in basics or blindly fallen in love and leaped in infatuated splendor towards axioms much like i did with my first childhood love who doesn't really talk to me anymore (lol you know who you are but it's fine it wouldn't really work out anyways, probably), we need to ground variation following through to global forms. the next step in one's journey is to name the mathematical machinery that was silently doing the work all along, or retroactively get competent in (meta)physical questions that were being answered in the first place by the math. mathematical physics is a structural companion to this journey: same eternal voice, but the camera turns toward categories, multilinear algebra, manifolds, cohomology, and the symplectic/Riemannian backbones that make the physics actually legible. (meta)physics still asks the questions; math is the terse answer key forced by those constraints.
