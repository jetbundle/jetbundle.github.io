symmetry, composition, variation

what picks out one trajectory among all mathematically conceivable paths through configuration space? why should the laws look the same if you translate the experiment, rotate it, or run it tomorrow instead of today? and if several such transformations leave the law unchanged, what is the correct notion of “doing one symmetry after another”—so that the algebra of redundancies is not ambiguous?

a provisional classical answer is that compatible dynamics arises from a single scalar quantity, the action, made stationary on the actual motion; the allowed symmetries then constrain the form of the lagrangian and, through noether’s theorem (developed later in geometric language), force conserved quantities along solutions. composition of symmetries is modeled by a group (or, when time reversal or coarse-graining is emphasized, first by a monoid); changes of description are morphisms in a category. none of this claims uniqueness of presentation: many lagrangians differ by total time derivatives yet yield the same equations; gauged and constrained systems hide redundant variables; quantum interference will reinterpret “allowed paths.” the point here is to fix the minimal classical spine—variation, linearized motion, and honest bookkeeping—before multilinear geometry carries the same ideas globally.

below, four threads are written out in full: (i) what symmetries are algebraically and how they act on configuration data; (ii) the principle of stationary action and the euler–lagrange equations, including conjugate momenta, boundary conditions, and explicit time dependence; (iii) linear superposition near equilibrium, normal modes, and the spectral distinction between symmetric stiffness/mass pairs and defective linearizations; (iv) categorical language as compression of “same physics, many coordinates,” with quotients and exact sequences as previews of reduction and cohomology. what remains open on purpose: whether least action is “really” teleological or emergent; how discrete and gauge symmetries alter the groupoid picture; and where the boundary between bookkeeping and ontology should be drawn when a functor re-packages the entire theory.

algebraic structure of symmetries

physics. a symmetry of a model is an active transformation of states or fields that carries solutions to solutions. examples: translating all particles by the same vector, rotating rigid frames, shifting the time origin for autonomous laws. if transformation \(g\) sends state \(x\) to \(g\cdot x\), then doing \(h\) after \(g\) must agree with a single transformation \(hg\); doing nothing is a symmetry; every physically allowed symmetry should be undoable in idealized reversible mechanics. that is exactly the group axioms in motion.

mathematics. a group \((G,\cdot)\) is a set with an associative product, an identity \(e\) with \(eg=ge=g\), and inverses \(g^{-1}\) with \(g^{-1}g=gg^{-1}=e\). a left action of \(G\) on a set \(Q\) (configuration space at this level of abstraction) is a map \((g,q)\mapsto g\cdot q\) such that \(e\cdot q=q\) and \((gh)\cdot q=g\cdot(h\cdot q)\). a symmetry of equations is often packaged as an action on \(Q\) that preserves the lagrangian up to a total time derivative (then the stationary-action principle is invariant); noether’s theorem, in a first pass, turns one-parameter subgroups \(\exp(s\xi)\subset G\) into conserved charges along trajectories.

monoid first. if irreversibility or coarse observation is primary, one may drop inverses: a monoid has associative product and identity only. classical conservative mechanics prefers groups because along a solution you can run time backward and stay in the same theory.

concrete groups. translations of \(\mathbb{R}^3\) form the additive group \((\mathbb{R}^3,+)\). rotations leaving lengths and orientations fixed form \(SO(3)\); including reflections gives \(O(3)\). galilean boosts, spatial rotations, translations, and time translations fit into the galilean group (often presented as a semidirect product); relativity replaces this with the poincaré group, deferred here. a homomorphism \(\varphi:G\to H\) between symmetry groups preserves composition: \(\varphi(gh)=\varphi(g)\varphi(h)\); changing coordinates or units should be such a morphism, not an arbitrary reshuffling.

what you should carry forward. name the group acting, distinguish global from local (gauge) symmetry in words, and recognize when a map of transformations fails to be a homomorphism (then “composition of changes of description” is ill-defined).

action, variation, euler–lagrange

setup. fix generalized coordinates \(q=(q^1,\ldots,q^n)\) on an open region of configuration space and consider trajectories \(q(t)\) for \(t\in[t_1,t_2]\). suppose a lagrangian \(L(q,\dot q,t)\) is given (in this section everything is coordinatewise; the geometric meaning of \(\partial L/\partial\dot q\) as a covector comes with manifolds later). the action is

\[ S[q] = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} L(q(t),\dot q(t),t)\,\mathrm{d}t . \]

principle. the physical path is a stationary point of \(S\) among all \(C^1\) variations \(q(t)+\delta q(t)\) with fixed endpoints \(\delta q(t_1)=\delta q(t_2)=0\). (local minima are common, but the first-order condition is stationarity.)

first variation. to first order, \(\delta\dot q=\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\delta q\). then

\[ \delta S = \int_{t_1}^{t_2}\left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial q^i}\,\delta q^i + \frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot q^i}\,\delta\dot q^i \right)\mathrm{d}t = \int_{t_1}^{t_2}\left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial q^i} - \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot q^i} \right)\delta q^i\,\mathrm{d}t, \]

after integration by parts, using vanishing boundary data so the boundary term \(\bigl[\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot q^i}\delta q^i\bigr]_{t_1}^{t_2}\) is zero. since the \(\delta q^i\) are arbitrary inside the interval, stationarity \(\delta S=0\) is equivalent to the euler–lagrange equations

\[ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot q^i} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q^i} = 0, \qquad i=1,\ldots,n. \]

conjugate momenta and hamiltonian preview. define \(p_i=\partial L/\partial\dot q^i\). when the legendre transform is well posed (hessian \(\partial^2 L/\partial\dot q^i\,\partial\dot q^j\) nondegenerate), the hamiltonian \(H=\sum_i p_i\dot q^i-L\) is a function of \((q,p,t)\) along solutions. if \(L\) has no explicit time dependence, \(\partial L/\partial t=0\) and formally \(\mathrm{d}H/\mathrm{d}t=0\) on solutions—energy conservation in autonomous systems. if \(L\) depends on \(t\) explicitly, energy need not be conserved; the same variation calculus applies, but first integrals come only from symmetries in the remaining variables.

boundary conditions. fixed endpoints select a dirichlet problem in path space; other physical statements (free ends, constraints) change boundary terms and hence the natural boundary conditions—those extensions belong to constrained calculus and later geometric formulations.

galilean hint. requiring invariance of the action of a free particle up to endpoint terms forces the kinetic part of \(L\) to depend on \(\dot q\) only through a quadratic form invariant under rotations; adding a potential \(V(q)\) breaks translation symmetry and destroys momentum conservation in that direction—consistent with the noether picture.

what you should carry forward. derive EL from \(S\) for any given \(L\); read off \(p_i\); decide whether energy is conserved from explicit \(t\) dependence; recognize equivalence of EL under \(L\mapsto L+\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}t\) for \(f(q,t)\).

superposition, modes, stability

linearization. suppose \(q_0\) is an equilibrium in generalized coordinates (all generalized forces vanish there). write \(q=q_0+\eta\) with \(\eta\) small, expand \(L\) to quadratic order in \(\eta,\dot\eta\) around static \(\eta=\dot\eta=0\). euler–lagrange linearize to a system of the form

\[ M\ddot\eta + K\eta = 0, \]

with symmetric positive-definite mass matrix \(M\) (kinetic term) and symmetric stiffness matrix \(K\) (potential curvature) in the common mechanical case. more generally, linearizations of nonconservative couplings produce first-order systems or nonsymmetric pairs \((M,K)\); the same spectral language applies with care.

normal modes. seek solutions \(\eta(t)=u\,\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\omega t}\) with \(u\in\mathbb{C}^n\). then \((K-\omega^2 M)u=0\). nontrivial \(u\) exist when \(\omega^2\) solves \(\det(K-\omega^2 M)=0\). for real symmetric \(M\) positive definite, there exists an \(M\)-orthogonal basis of real modes diagonalizing both quadratics; in coordinates aligned with normal modes, the system decouples into independent harmonic oscillators whose squared frequencies are the generalized eigenvalues \(\omega_k^2\).

stability. if all \(\omega_k^2>0\), small disturbances oscillate boundedly (elliptic equilibrium). if some \(\omega_k^2<0\), there are exponential runaway directions (hyperbolic instability). zero eigenvalues signal neutral modes—often related to continuous symmetry or redundancy—and demand reduced variables or dirac-style analysis later.

defective matrices. when \(M^{-1}K\) is not diagonalizable (nontrivial jordan blocks), normal modes alone do not span: there appear secular terms such as \(t\,\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\omega t}\) in the real-time solution. physically, repeated frequencies with insufficient geometric multiplicity produce polynomial growth in degenerate subspaces; contrast this with the symmetric case where repeated eigenvalues can still admit a full orthogonal basis of modes (think isotropic oscillator shells versus deliberately tuned nonsymmetric couplings).

superposition. linearity implies that sums of solutions are solutions; the general small motion is a superposition of modes (plus jordan contributions if present). this is the backbone of acoustics, molecular vibrations, and stability analysis before chaos enters.

what you should carry forward. assemble \(M,K\) from a lagrangian; solve the generalized eigenproblem; classify stability; recognize when jordan form intrudes and what it does to long-time growth.

bookkeeping across descriptions

problem. one and the same rod, fluid, or solar system can be described in lab frames, body-fixed frames, center-of-mass plus relative coordinates, or fourier modes. which structures must be preserved when passing between descriptions, and how is “quotienting out” a symmetry (e.g. translation invariance) not mere notation?

categories, informally. a category has objects (types of models: spaces, groups, vector spaces) and morphisms (structure-preserving maps: smooth maps, homomorphisms, linear maps) that compose associatively and have identities. a functor sends objects and morphisms of one category to another while respecting composition—linearization is a functor from a category of pointed mechanical systems to vector spaces; forgetting extra structure is another functor.

products and coproducts (words). the product of two objects encodes paired independent data (joint system with projections to each factor). the coproduct encodes disjoint alternative or free combination (direct sum of vector spaces is both product and coproduct in finite dimensions—a special accident). universal properties pin these constructions uniquely up to unique isomorphism, which is the honest replacement for hand-waving “choose coordinates.”

quotients and normal subgroups. if a group \(G\) acts on configuration space \(Q\) and physical observables are constant on orbits, the reduced space is often a quotient \(Q/G\) (after resolving singularities carefully). if \(H\triangleleft G\) is a normal subgroup, \(G/H\) is a group capturing residual symmetries after modding out \(H\); this is the algebraic face of “remove center of mass motion” or “factor out gauge.”

exact sequences as preview. a short exact sequence \(0\to A\to B\to C\to 0\) means \(B\) is an extension of \(C\) by \(A\): every element of \(C\) lifts to \(B\) with ambiguity in \(A\). the same diagrammatic tension reappears as closed versus exact differential forms in cohomology—obstructions to global potentials are bookkeeping of “which lifts are consistent.”

what you should carry forward. articulate a change of model as a morphism or functor; recognize when a quotient is legitimate (free, proper actions; or stratified geometry); read a short exact sequence as obstruction data, not mere symbols.
