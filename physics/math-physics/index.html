mathematical physics

a structural companion to classical physics

while the classical spine is laid out in basics—from variational principles through symmetries, reduction, linearization, phase space, special relativity, waves and radiation, general relativity, and global topological closure—this page enumerates the mathematical methods that make those structures possible. the work of mathematics is to characterize problems and their underlying shape with precision; physics poses the constraints, and the minimal imaginative language follows. each algebraic object, invariant, and operator here exists because the classical story demanded it. this is a layered blueprint: navigable below, cross-linked back to the corresponding classical passages where it helps.

algebraic substrate and categorical foundations

combining states, comparing observables, and changing frames forces groups (reversible, associative symmetries), vector spaces (superposition), and categories (objects like configuration or phase spaces, morphisms as processes, functors as change of description). normal subgroups and quotients are the algebraic face of gauge redundancy. linearity hands you matrices, spectra, and jordan blocks when operators refuse diagonalization. this layer fixes what naive sets miss about reversibility and structural comparison—the floor under everything in variational calculus and symmetries (mechanics, landau & lifshitz vol. 1).

metric and multilinear structures

measuring lengths, angles, and (where relevant) probabilities asks for bilinear forms: euclidean geometry from symmetric positive-definite data and gram–schmidt; minkowski signature and light cones when indefiniteness is physical; skew-symmetric nondegenerate forms forcing symplectic geometry—the natural home of phase space and poisson structure. tensor products carry multilinearity; symmetrization and alternation feed the exterior algebra and the wedge product. this is the linear infrastructure behind variation, quadratic reduction, and canonical brackets.

differentiable manifolds and the calculus of forms

configuration space and spacetime are rarely one global chart. manifolds patch euclidean pieces with smooth transitions; tangent bundles carry velocities, cotangent bundles carry momenta and differentials. partitions of unity weld local constructions into global ones. exterior forms unify line and surface data; the exterior derivative \(\mathrm{d}\) satisfies \(\mathrm{d}^2=0\) and packages gradient, curl, and divergence without coordinates. lie derivatives and frobenius classify symmetries and holonomic constraints. this is the language that makes curved spacetime and global maxwell on manifolds honest.

cohomological invariants and spectral topology

local exactness does not imply global exactness. de rham cohomology measures closed versus exact forms—obstructions to global potentials, dirac strings, monopole thought experiments. mayer–vietoris and čech–de rham compute; poincaré duality pairs degrees on oriented manifolds. for bundles, thom and euler classes detect twists and missing global sections; spectral sequences organize fibrations. this is the topological side of gauge redundancy and flux questions already touched in global and topological closure.

the kinematics and dynamics of classical mechanics

configuration space as a manifold with generalized coordinates; action on the tangent bundle forcing euler–lagrange equations; noether pairing symmetries to conserved quantities; legendre map to the cotangent bundle, hamilton equations, and poisson brackets from the symplectic form; hamilton–jacobi as generating-function technology; action–angle variables and adiabatic invariants for integrable sectors; groupoids and topology for global histories. the classical tour in variation, reduction, linearization, and phase space is the concrete instance of this layer.

relativistic spacetime and electrodynamics

finite invariant speed forces minkowski geometry: indefinite metric, light cones, lorentz transformations, four-vectors for coordinates and energy–momentum. the electromagnetic field is the 2-form \(F=\mathrm{d}A\); maxwell splits into the identity \(\mathrm{d}F=0\) and the sourced equation \(\mathrm{d}\star F=\star J\) with the hodge star in lorentz signature. retarded and liénard–wiechert potentials enforce causality; the symmetric stress tensor encodes local conservation. see special relativity and coupling, waves and radiation, and forms closure.

geometric gravity, connections, and curvature

gravity as geometry: lorentzian manifolds, the levi-civita connection (metric-compatible, torsion-free), riemann tensor measuring holonomy of parallel transport, einstein–hilbert action and field equations \(G_{ik}\sim T_{ik}\), schwarzschild as the spherical vacuum, geodesics and perihelion precession. this is the mathematical closure of the story told in general relativity and curved spacetime.
