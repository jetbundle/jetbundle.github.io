# Resources

A collection of computational tools, interactive modules, and structured learning paths.

## Start Here: Differential Equations

The **language of mathematical physics** begins with differential equations.

**Recommendation**: Start with [A Survey of Differential Equations and their Algebraic Geometry](/diffequations/) (Infrastructure in development)

This complete modern treatment covers:
- Classical theory (ODEs, PDEs)
- Stochastic analysis (SDEs, SPDEs)
- Geometric methods (rough paths, regularity structures)

**Why start here**: Differential equations are the foundation for understanding:
- Physics (motion, fields, waves)
- Engineering (control systems, continuum mechanics)
- Computational science (numerical methods, simulation)
- Financial engineering (pricing, risk management)

## The Learning Path

**Foundations first**: Mathematics and physics provide the essential structure for understanding modern systems.

1. **Master differential equations** - The language of change and dynamics
2. **Understand computational methods** - How to simulate andd calculate reality
3. **Apply to engineering** - Build systems that work

## Why This Order Matters

**Computers don't create understanding** - they execute instructions.

**Modern financial systems** - were engineered by mathematicians and physicists.

**True capability** comes from:
- Knowing the mathematical structure and physical principles first
- Then learning how to compute with it
- Finally applying it to real problems
